Fast Reinforcement Learning with Tactile Sensing (Fast-Touch-RL)

Zhehan Li1, Alice Chen1, Bob Wang2
1Tongverse Robotics Lab, 2Institute of Intelligent Machines
Paper (PDF) arXiv Video Code Data

A fast, robust RL framework that leverages realistic foot tactile sensing for contact-rich locomotion and manipulation.

Abstract

We present Fast-Touch-RL, a reinforcement learning framework that integrates realistic foot tactile perception into training for contact-rich robotics. Our method measures ground reaction forces and center-of-pressure signals in simulation and aligns them with physically plausible distributions at the robot's feet. By conditioning the policy on these tactile cues and shaping rewards with contact-aware objectives, the agent learns stable and agile locomotion substantially faster than baselines that ignore tactile sensing. We evaluate on uneven terrains and running scenarios, demonstrating improved sample efficiency, contact stability, and robustness to domain randomization.
